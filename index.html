About The Conference

This conference brings together leading experts and healthcare professionals to explore the latest advances in the diagnosis, treatment, and multidisciplinary management of immune-mediated inflammatory diseases (IMIDs). With a focus on clinical practice, pharmacological updates, genetic insights, and innovative imaging techniques, the sessions are designed to enhance participant knowledge across dermatology, rheumatology, gastroenterology, hepatology, and beyond. Through a series of targeted learning objectives, attendees will gain practical skills in patient monitoring, differential diagnosis, and integrated care planning for complex IMID cases.

Conference Objectives

  • Identify three common dermatologic signs of IBD and relate them to underlying inflammatory mechanisms
  • Differentiate between two biologic treatments for IMIDs in terms of efficacy and safety
  • Design a monitoring plan to detect adverse effects of biologic therapies across organ systems
  • Outline how genetic and epigenetic factors influence IMID progression with at least two examples
  • Describe two interdisciplinary strategies for managing uveitis in IMID patients
  • Formulate a management approach for autoimmune hepatitis coexisting with primary sclerosing cholangitis
  • Explain the role of two advanced imaging techniques in diagnosing and managing joint inflammation
  • Evaluate the roles and mechanisms of two biologic inhibitors (e.g., TNF, IL-17, JAK) in treating IMIDs
  • Distinguish between at least three subcategories of seronegative spondy loarthritis
  • Propose a multidisciplinary care plan for the effective management of psoriatic arthritis
